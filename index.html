Structure, progressions & coaching cues

PROGRAM STRUCTURE

Warm-Up (1 set): Non-negotiable before every session. These four movements prime your spine, core, and hips before you load them. Never skip the warm-up â it's where injury prevention lives.

Main Lift (2â3 sets): The primary training block. Start at 2 sets and build to 3 as you adapt. Rest 60â90 seconds between sets. Focus on quality movement over load â especially on single-leg work.

Accessory (2 sets): Finishers for arm health and muscle balance. Keep rest short (30â45 sec). These should feel like a burnout â controlled, not sloppy.

PROGRESSIONS

Weeks 1â2: Learn the patterns. Use light loads. The cable split squat and SL RDL will feel awkward â that's normal. Nail the positions before adding weight.

Weeks 3â4: Start adding load to the main lift. Half kneeling press and row should feel stable â if you're wobbling or rotating, reduce the weight.

Weeks 5+: Increase load by 5% per week when you hit the top of the rep range with clean form. Add a third set to main lifts if recovery allows.

KEY COACHING CUES

Cable Split Squat: "Knee down, not forward." Let the back knee drop straight to the floor rather than driving it forward over the back toe.

Half Kneeling: "Hips square, glute on." Your kneeling side glute should be switched on the entire set. If your hip drifts, you've lost the position.

SL RDL: "Push the floor away." Think about pressing your standing foot into the ground rather than pulling yourself up. Smoother reps, better muscle tension.

General: Control the eccentric (lowering) phase on every rep. Slowing down the negative adds muscle, improves joint health, and reveals weaknesses in your technique.

Program structure reflects cable-based functional training principles. Always consult your trainer before modifying loads or progressions.
